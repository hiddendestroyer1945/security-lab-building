* **Introduction**: It explains the different methods used to collect passive information about an organization from the software test platform in the IT Security Lab network, the software programs used for that, how should a security professional think about for collecting passive information about an organization??, and how to build a profile of that organization using the information available.

* **Information Gathering**: Information gathering is the act of collecting data for a specific purpose. That is, data is collected to profiling a specific target and the information in that data is used to attack.
* The attacker searches the Internet to see if there is any information available that could be used to attack any of the organization's assets, while the security expert checks the Internet to see if has made any information available on the Internet that could be used to attack.

* **Starting at the Source**: The search for information about a company starts from the company's website. The About Us section on the company's website will contain information about the company's name, its executive members, and its holdings.
* It is difficult to directly attack the networks of large organizations. But, when small organizations merge into larger organizations, more emphasis is placed on connectivity between the networks of the two organizations than on security, so the security between these two networks is low, and therefore the networks of this larger organization can be attacked through the networks of the smaller organization. Therefore, it is advisable to exclude information about such mergers from the website and the Internet.
* If information about the physical locations of organizations' offices is available, it can be used to carry out the specified attacks. Therefore, it is best to avoid such information from websites and the Internet.

* **Location Information Based Attacks**: If information about the physical location of organizations' offices is available, that information can be used to specified types of attacks. Such attacks are explained here.
  * **Dumpster Diving**: Dumpster diving is the act of searching for information about a company in the dumpsters of its offices. Dumpster diving is a type of attack that is used to collect information about a company's employees, such as their names, addresses, and phone numbers. But sometimes operation manuals and configuration guides and financial documents may be available from such dumpsters.
  * To combat such attacks, it is important to ensure that all data dumped from organizations' offices to external dumpsters is destroyed before being dumped into such dumpsters.
  * **Wardriving**: Wardriving is the process of discovering wireless networks in a company's offices and the status of these wireless networks. Wardriving involves discovering wireless networks in a company's offices and discovering networks and computers that are directly connected to the Internet using these wireless networks.
